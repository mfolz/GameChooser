Overwhelmed by the 15 games playing on NBA League Pass tonight? Stuck at work and want to choose a game to record on your DVR? New to watching basketball and have no idea what to watch? GameChooser was made for you!

GameChooser suggests the best NBA games to watch on a given night, based on the simple idea that people:

  1. like to watch their favorite teams,
  2. prefer to watch good teams over bad teams,
  3. prefer to watch competitive games,
  4. prefer to watch high-scoring games

The end user provides the information for the first factor, the information for the second factor is provided by checking the season standings, and the third and fourth factors are estimated by a machine learning model which predicts the outcome of all NBA games on a given night. These factors are distilled into a measure of watchability called the GameChooser Score.

The machine learning model at the core of GameChooser uses 20 factors to predict the outcome of NBA games, and was trained on NBA games from 2006 onwards. This model uses win-loss records, point differentials, home-away splits, scheduling, and performance over trailing time intervals of various length to assess the strength of a team at a given date. It uses random forest classifiers to predict winners and estimate win probabilities, and support vector regression to estimate point differentials and predict the number of points scored for each team.

In addition to providing intelligent viewing recommendations, GameChooser predicts the outcome of all NBA games on a given night, provides advice for betting the spread and over/under, and generates weekly rankings of NBA teams.
